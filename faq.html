We are a private space company that specializes in virtual events. Our unique combination of in-person launches and virtual broadcasts allows us to reach customers around the world. We believe in the importance of opportunity, and aim to offer something for everyone. We conduct research both in space and on the ground, and love making new discoveries.
Private space flights like Phoenix offer the benefits of space travel to those who can afford it. However, unlike public space flights, private companies are not required to release information about the cost of every flight or share their flight data with the government which could eventually lead to unethical practices. Private space flights allow for more flexibility and confidentiality, benefitting the customers.
The FAA oversees Phoenix’s private space flights. The FAA's Office of Commercial Space Transportation is the office that regulates the launch and reentry of commercial space vehicles including spacecraft, satellites, or other objects issued a launch or reentry license by the FAA.
As published by the Space Foundation in The Space Report 2021 Q2, the global space economy in 2020 was $446.9 billion, up 4.4% from 2019 and up 55% for the decade. This annual report from the Space Foundation tracks worldwide spending by governments, the private sector and consumers. Space transportation is an enabling capability, one that makes it possible to send national security and commercial satellites into orbit, probes into the solar system, and humans on exploration missions.
Phoenix is unique because we offer both virtual as well as in-person options. Customers are able to interact with other space enthusiasts and even speak with astronauts on missions. Private spaceflight is an emerging industry that has the potential to expand human activity beyond Earth. Private spaceflight is less risky than commercial missions because it is funded by private companies and not government funding. This means that if an accident occurs, the company takes responsibility for all costs, but if something goes wrong on a commercial mission, the government pays for all costs of damages or injuries. In addition, private missions are usually more cost-effective than commercial missions because they require less initial investment of time and money by the private company. Accordingly, private spaceflight tends to be more innovative than traditional commercial missions because of the variety of private companies involved with different backgrounds and interests who are willing to take more aggressive risks.
Like many other private launch companies, Phoenix operates out of various launch sites, most notably Cape Canaveral in Florida as well as the Vandenberg Air Force Base in California. Both of these sites are government-owned launch facilities and used for launches of government-owned satellites. Private and government launches can also take place from any one of the 13 other non-federal launch sites, or "spaceports".
