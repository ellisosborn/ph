Spaceships

Zeus - Launch Vehicle

Named after the god of the sky, Zeus is a multi-configuration extremely heavy-lift launch vehicle capable of consistently carrying payloads to earth orbits and outside of it. It features the most powerful rocket engine ever created - The in-house Whirlpool engine.

The Zeus rocket has a reusable first stage, enabling multiple launches in a short amount of time. The low price of the launch vehicle and ridesharing configurations will allow a single configuration launch vehicle capable of carrying both people and payloads to orbit. Zeus will build a road to space, featuring a reusable first stage built for 25 missions.

Engine: Vortex
Spaceships

Kronos - Liquid Fuel Rocket

A second addition to the Phoenix space fleet, Kronos utilizes similar ascent and propulsion stages. Kronos combines our previous development with a second stage aero compartment, which combines the lift of an airplane and the power of a rocket. This second stage compartment has patented aero-wings which increase efficiency by up to 25% over the previous iteration of Phoenix’s rockets.

As well as the Zeus engine, the Pegasus launch vehicle utilizes the Vortex engines. In contrast to the extremely heavy-lift launch vehicle Zeus, the Pegasus offers a lower budget and smaller size in order for the smaller size business or individual to have a platform to launch to space.

Engine: Vortex
