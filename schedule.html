History

Past Launch Dates

Blast into the past as you explore our previous launches starting from the end of 2012 and leading up until present day

11

December, 2012St. Augustine, Florida

First Test Launch

  • First attempt to send a Phoenix rocket beyond Tropospheric levels. The remainder of the prototype series will take place over the next six months in various locations throughout the United States. This flight will be unmanned, but Phoenix plans to have passengers aboard within the next seven years.

5

May, 2013Cape Canaveral, Florida

First Rocket to Orbit

  • The first successful launch and orbit of the Zeus rocket was accomplished on May 5th, 2013. This project was a joint venture and the goal of this project is to resupply the International Space Station as well as provide other satellite services such as weather satellites, spy satellites, etc. The Zeus has been in development for many years and it’s finally ready to be used commercially.

9

August, 2014Kodiak Island, Alaska

First Recovery of an Orbiting Rocket

  • Phoenix launched its prototype rocket, which carried a single Geostationary Satellite to be utilized for tracking and videography for future virtual events. The launch was initially planned for November 2013, but had to be postponed due to a helium leak on the rocket's second stage.

8

March, 2015Wallops Island, Virginia

First Rocket Docked on ISS

  • Phoenix docked onto the International Space Station (ISS) using the Calypso mounting dock. The ISS was constructed by the U.S., Canada, Russia, Japan, and eleven other members of the ISS program who participate in space exploration activities there. This markates a crucial milestone for Phoenix and our company plans to assist in geopositioning research.

24

November, 2016Boca Chica, Texas

First Satellite to LEO

  • Phoenix’s first satellite, named the Chimera, was launched on November 24th, 2015 and reached low earth orbit in a little less than 2 hours. This satellite will be used to investigate planets and moons in our solar system as well as asteroids, comets, and Kuiper Belt objects.

18

August, 2017Lompoc, California

First Self-Landing

  • Phoenix’s first self-landing mission was an amazing step towards decreasing the cost of space travel and exploration. This milestone is monumental in enabling new missions that were not previously possible.

17

December, 2018Cocoa Beach, Florida

First Human Spaceflight

  • In December 2018, Phoenix successfully sent humans into space. With this new breakthrough, we hope that in the future we will be able to send more people into space and even send them into deep space. The next prospective missions include testing new technologies, doing atmospheric tests, researching cosmic radiation, reaching the moon, and studying how human physiology changes when it enters low-gravity conditions.

2

July, 2019Van Horn, Texas

Vortex and Kronos Revealed

  • The Vortex rocket engine was revealed as the first of its kind. Designed to be able to produce thrust without the need for a nozzle, huge amounts of waste heat are avoided. The Kronos launch vehicle is designed to carry heavy payloads up into orbit. It can launch satellites or other payloads into space with relative ease.

4

March, 2021St. Augustine, Florida

Vortex Fully Tested

  • The Vortex rocket engine is fully tested and ready for private use. Vortex is the most powerful liquefied natural gas fueled rocket engine ever developed. Using Atomic metallic hydrogen, the Vortex rocket engine is capable of producing over eleven thousand kN (2,500,000lbf) of thrust with throttling capability.

13

August, 2021Jacksonville, Florida

First Rocket Reused

  • The first reusable rocket launch in August 2021 was a huge success. It was the first time a rocket has been reused for a commercial mission and will save Phoenix a lot of money as they plan more missions.
