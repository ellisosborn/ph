About Phoenix

Our Story



Companies are made up of people with a dream and a common goal. In pursuit of the unknown, our founders came together and combined our passions and knowledge to form Phoenix. We desperately wanted to leave our cubicle jobs and pursue something bigger than ourselves. Similar dreams of space exploration and adventure brought the team together from all corners of the United States in an incredibly unique way. After joining a public group call meant for those curious about space, four individuals focused on astrophysics and engineering clicked and formed an instant connection. Despite diverse backgrounds and experiences, we were united under the one goal of “crossing the final frontier.”



Phoenix’s are known for rising out of the ashes and gaining new life. Similarly, our team rose from nothing and gained a new life once we took up our passions and created Phoenix Space Exploration. We targeted those who had a hidden burning passion for space, but were just too afraid to pursue it as we were all in that position once ourselves. Although we started as a company with customers, we hoped to become something that resembled an open door family.



With the assistance from aerospace engineers and space industry professionals, we were able to develop our Zeus and Vortex models and begin preparation for an interactive website. After months of testing, Phoenix went live and then had its first public launch. Unfortunately, our timing was poor as a global pandemic took the world by storm. The pandemic affected millions of companies worldwide, but we were determined to keep our dream alive. Much like the formation of the company itself, we were able to persevere and continue following our dreams. Virtual launches began, and unique interactive Virtual Reality experiences were released to the public. Instantly, anyone from any corner of the globe could experience a real life space flight with their own eyes.

Giving Back to the Community


Flying Phoenix Initiative


- Philanthropy at Phoenix



Our Flying Phoenix Initiative program seeks out both academically excelling and genuinely curious students, bringing them onto our campus for summer mentorship that places them side by side with Phoenix engineers and scientists. From mission control to outer space, students contribute to work that matters with a company where diversity, equity, and inclusion are shared values. We're committed to fostering an environment for every teammate that's welcoming, respectful, and inclusive, with great opportunities for professional growth.

Students are assigned to individual mentors during this eight-week program and are tasked with collaborative projects, including mock shuttle visualization and construction using industry-level technology. At the end of their experience, acclaimed scientists and aerospace engineers decide upon a group of five members, based on the quality and originality of the student's final project, to fly 25 kilometers into the stratosphere using one of Phoenix's original shuttles.
