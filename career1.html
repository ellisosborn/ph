Careers

Aerospace Engineer


The Aerospace Engineer is responsible for designing, developing, and testing aircraft and spacecraft. This includes determining aviation and space-related operational requirements and designing aircraft and spacecraft components, subsystems, and systems such as wings, fuselage, landing gear, propulsion systems, flight controls, navigation systems.

Responsibilities:


  • - Designing aerospace vehicles, including airplanes and spacecraft

  • - Developing plans for new designs such as specifications for materials such as the dimensions of the wings or the size of the engines

  • - Testing aircraft or spacecraft to see if they can withstand forces such as wind or gravity that may be stronger than expected

  • - Designing models to test how well an airplane or spacecraft can withstand forces like wind or gravity

  • - Developing software for testing new designs





Astrophysicist


The Astrophysicist is responsible for researching, developing, and analyzing astrophysical phenomena. Research includes observation, measurement, and interpretation of data collected from sources such as telescopes or satellites. Development involves using computers to create models or simulations to understand understand astronomical phenomena better. The analysis includes concluding from existing data in order to determine how it supports or contradicts existing theories.

Responsibilities:


  • - Research and analyze information collected by observatories and satellites

  • - Analyze data in order to support or contradict existing theories

  • - Develop computer models to understand astronomical phenomena better

  • -Use telescopes or satellites to collect data about astronomical phenomenon

  • - Support Aerospace team in developing rocket testing software
